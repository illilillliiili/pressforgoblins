about

yes hello this is noah. please ignore the shifting height. nothing to see here. we i am the founder of an editorial collective operating out of a dimly-lit, well-guarded workshop in london, specialising in sci-fi, fantasy, horror, romance and speculative fiction. the stranger, the better.

note: noah might actually be these three goblins in a trenchcoat:

editorial goblin

sharpens what's already there. dissects pacing, character arcs, tension, stakes, themes and emotion to make sure your story feels right. spots plot holes before they swallow your manuscript. handles the practical stuff: developmental editing, copyediting, line editing, proofreading and second reading.

creative goblin

brings fresh ideas, new angles and the momentum you need when you're stuck. plot twists, new characters, hooks and possibilities you never saw coming. might ask bizarre questions at you until something clicks. always curious, always enthusiastic.

marketing goblin

knows what agents and publishers want to see and what the market is hungry for. pitches, straplines, brand identity and positioning. makes projects impossible to ignore. his one-liners hit like a well-timed goblin brick.

where are you on your journey?

the journey from a blank page to a published book is a long crawl. identify your current level to begin.

vibe check
[no gold required]

a free 15-minute recon chat. tell us about your world, your struggles or that one scene you cannot stop thinking about. we will see if we are the right goblins for the job.

prologue
[ideas & brainstorming]

wandering the map, sketching characters and unearthing lore. the creative goblin helps you find the sharpest path and the ideas worth chasing.

levelling up
[editorial development]

you are in the thick of it, but the light is fading. the plot goblin moves in to fix the pacing and ensure your stakes land where they need to.

inventory check
[manuscript assessment]

the draft is finished. now you need an honest read from someone who knows what agents and publishers are looking for. you get: a detailed editorial letter covering structure, pacing, character development and marketability, plus a redlined manuscript with specific feedback.

secret level
[custom strategy]

your project is a strange beast that needs a bespoke strategy from all three in the trenchcoat. tell us your weirdest requirements.

final boss
[pitching & submission]

the pitch goblin crafts query letters and one-liners that hit like a thrown goblin brick to help you land the agent. you get: a polished query package ready to submit.

we quote based on manuscript length, timeline and complexity. start with a free vibe check and we can settle on a gold payment that works for you and the goblins.
authors from a background traditionally underrepresented in publishing will receive a discounted rate.

what the goblins love

• speculative fiction, science fiction, fantasy, horror and romantasy
• middle grade, young adult, crossover and new adult
• graphic novels and comic books
• technical lore like board and card game guides, video game scripts and narrative design

the goblins are not the right fit for literary fiction (which often lulls them into a deep sleep), poetry or non-fiction (unless it involves other fantastical creatures like them)
