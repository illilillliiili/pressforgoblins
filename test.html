where are you on the map?

the journey from a blank page to a published book is a long crawl. identify your current level to begin.

  • vibe check [no gold required]

    a free 15-minute recon chat. tell us about your world, your struggles, or that one scene you cannot stop thinking about. we will see if we are the right goblins for the job.

  • prologue [ideas & brainstorming]

    wandering the map, sketching characters, and unearthing lore. the creative goblin helps you find the sharpest path and the ideas worth chasing.

  • the deep haul [editorial development]

    you are in the thick of it, but the light is fading. the plot goblin moves in to fix the pacing and ensure your stakes feel lethal.

  • the bone inspection [manuscript assessment]

    the draft is finished. before you head out, the plot goblin inspects the architecture while the pitch goblin looks for holes in your armour.

  • secret level [custom strategy]

    rare encounters. your project is a strange beast that needs a bespoke strategy from all three in the trenchcoat. tell us your weirdest requirements.

  • final boss [pitching & submission]

    entering the arena. the pitch goblin crafts query letters and one-liners that hit like a thrown goblin brick to help you land the agent.

all quests are quoted individually based on manuscript length and complexity. start with a free vibe check to discuss your project and get a quote that works for you and the goblins.

choosing your allies

what the goblins love what the goblins avoid
speculative fiction: sff, horror, and romantasy. literary fiction: if it is allergic to plot, we are not for you.
ya crossover & middle grade: action and adventure. poetry: respect to the bards, but it is not our craft.
visual storytelling: graphic novels and comic scripts. pure romance: unless there is magic, murder, or monsters involved.
worldbuilding: magic systems, board games, and card games. memoir: unless you are a time traveller or a vampire.
technical lore: board game manuals and video game scripts. anything too serious: we like stories that know how to have fun.

our philosophy: honest critique over easy praise
